Research

  • Attention (And Money) Is All You Need: Why Universities Are Struggling to Keep AI Talent 2026
    with Akcigit, Chikis, and Dinlersoz
    Documents the shift of AI talent from academia to the private sector, driven in part by enormous wage top-end wage premia.
  • Measuring Artificial Intelligence Use by Businesses 2025
    with Dinlersoz, Foster, Haltiwanger
    CES 2024 Annual Report
    Documents how the Census Bureau measures business AI use, synthesizing early evidence across surveys and administrative data.
  • Growth is Getting Harder to Find, Not Ideas 2025
    with Fort, Liang, Schott, Zolas
    Patents per R&D dollar and growth per patent are not declining; slower growth reflects factors beyond ideas.
  • Early Joiners and Startup Performance 2025
    with Choi, Haltiwanger, Kim
    Review of Economics and Statistics
    Non-founder early employees are pivotal to startup success, and losing one has long-run negative effects.
    Appendix
  • Firm Growth and Stagnation in the United States: Key Trends and New Data Opportunities 2025
    with Kim, Choi, Haltiwanger
    Strategic Management Journal
    Introduces new public data showing the dynamics of high-growth and stagnating firms.
  • A Simulated Reconstruction and Reidentification Attack on the 2010 U.S. Census 2025
    with Abowd, Adams, Ashmead, Darais, Dey, Garfinkel, Kifer, Leclerc, Lew, Moore, Rodriguez, Tadros, Vilhuber
    Harvard Data Science Review
    Demonstrates that published tables enabled large-scale reconstruction of confidential 2010 Census records.
  • Measuring the Characteristics and Employment Dynamics of U.S. Inventors 2025
    with Akcigit
    Journal of Economic Growth
    Creates linked microdata to document who inventors are, where they work, and what they earn.
  • The Impact of AI on the Workforce: Tasks versus Jobs? 2024
    with Bonney, Breaux, Buffington, Dinlersoz, Foster, Haltiwanger, Kroff, Savage
    Economics Letters
    AI adoption is reshaping tasks far more than it is reducing employment.
  • Tracking Firm Use of AI in Real Time: A Snapshot from the Business Trends and Outlook Survey 2024
    with Bonney, Breaux, Buffington, Dinlersoz, Foster, Haltiwanger, Kroff, Savage
    Real-time survey data show rapid but still limited AI diffusion with modest employment effects.
  • Advanced Technology Adoption: Selection or Causal Effects? 2023
    with Acemoglu, Anderson, Beede, Buffington, Childress, Dinlersoz, Foster, Haltiwanger, Kroff, Restrepo, Zolas
    AEA Papers and Proceedings
    Large firms adopt automation because they are already large and growing, not because adoption causes growth.
  • An In-Depth Examination of Requirements for Disclosure Risk Assessment 2023
    with Jarmin, Abowd, Ashmead, Cumings-Menon, Hawes, Keller, Kifer, Leclerc, Reiter, Rodriguez, Schmutte, Velkoff, Zhuravlev
    Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences
    No disclosure risk framework satisfies all desiderata, but counterfactual approaches perform well.
  • On The Role of Trademarks: From Micro Evidence to Macro Outcomes 2023
    with Dinlersoz, Yorukoglu, Zolas
    Trademarks raise variety, quality, and welfare, while also increasing market concentration.
  • Where Have All the "Creative Talents" Gone? Employment Dynamics of US Inventors 2023
    with Akcigit
    Inventors are shifting to large incumbents, earning more but producing fewer innovations.
  • Robot Hubs: The Skewed Distribution of Robots in U.S. Manufacturing 2023
    with Brynjolfsson, Buffington, Li, Miranda, Seamans
    AEA Papers and Proceedings
    Robot adoption is highly concentrated geographically in a small set of manufacturing hubs.
  • The Characteristics and Geographic Distribution of Robot Hubs in U.S. Manufacturing Establishments 2023
    with Brynjolfsson, Buffington, Li, Miranda, Seamans
    Robotics intensity is driven by establishment size and clustered in specialized local ecosystems.
  • Automation and the Workforce: A Firm-Level View from the 2019 Annual Business Survey 2022
    with Acemoglu, Anderson, Beede, Buffington, Childress, Dinlersoz, Foster, Haltiwanger, Kroff, Restrepo, Zolas
    Automation is uneven across firms and accounts for 20-30% of the higher labor productivity of large firms in an industry.
  • Redesigning the Longitudinal Business Database 2021
    with Chow, Fort, Goetz, Lawrence, Perlman, Stinson, White
    Details the redesign of the LBD, improving measurement of U.S. firm entry, exit, and growth.
  • Research Experience as Human Capital in New Business Outcomes 2021
    with Jarmin, Lane, Zolas
    Measuring and Accounting for Innovation in the 21st Century (book chapter)
    High-R&D human capital individuals select into riskier startups with "up-or-out" dynamics.
  • An Anatomy of U.S. Firms Seeking Trademark Registration 2021
    with Dinlersoz, Myers, Zolas
    Measuring and Accounting for Innovation in the 21st Century (book chapter)
    Trademark filing predicts subsequent firm growth and complements patent-based innovation measures.
  • Advanced Technologies Adoption and Use by U.S. Firms: Evidence from the Annual Business Survey 2020
    with Zolas, Kroff, Brynjolfsson, McElheran, Beede, Buffington, Foster, Dinlersoz
    Frontier technologies are rare but concentrated in large firms, amplifying worker exposure.
  • Business Dynamics of High Tech Industries 2020
    with Miranda
    Journal of Economics & Management Strategy
    Introduces new BDS tables for High Tech industries, showing their outsized role in job creation.
  • Tracking the Technological Composition of Industries with Algorithmic Patent Concordances 2019
    with Lybbert, Zolas
    Economics of Innovation and New Technology
    Builds patent-industry concordances to better measure technological change across sectors.
  • Proximity and Economic Activity: An Analysis of Vendor-University Transactions 2019
    with Lane, Weinberg, Zolas
    Journal of Regional Science
    Research spending is locally concentrated and deepens firm-university linkages.
  • Squeezing More Out of Your Data: Business Record Linkage with Python 2018
    with Cuffe
    Introduces scalable machine-learning tools for high-quality business record linkage.
  • Is Federal Regulation to Blame for the Decline in American Entrepreneurship? 2018
    with Tabarrok
    Economic Policy
    Regulatory changes do not explain the broad-based decline in business dynamism.
  • Just Passing Through: Characterizing U.S. Pass-Through Business Owners 2017
    with Kim, McCue
    Uses administrative tax data to profile pass-through business owners and their firms.
  • Wrapping it up in a person: Examining employment and earnings outcomes for Ph.D. recipients 2015
    with Zolas, Jarmin, Stephan, Owen-Smith, Rosen, Allen, Weinberg, Lane
    Science, 350(6266), 1367–1371
    Documents the flow of Ph.D.s on federally funded grants to high-wage industry roles, especially in High Tech sectors.
  • Is Entrepreneurship in Decline? 2015
    with Tabarrok
    Understanding the Growth Slowdown, Cato Institute Press (book chapter)
    Declining churn has many faces, some less worrisome than others.
